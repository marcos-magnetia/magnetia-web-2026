# B2B & B2C Copywriting Skill for Lead Generation

## Description
Use this skill when writing copy for websites, landing pages, service pages, emails, or any marketing content aimed at generating leads (citas, reservas, formularios) for B2B services or B2C local businesses.

**Triggers:**
- Creating landing pages for lead generation
- Writing website copy for service businesses
- Crafting email campaigns (cold outreach or nurture)
- Developing service descriptions or "about us" pages
- Creating CTAs, microcopy, or form optimization
- Keywords: "copywriting", "landing page", "lead gen", "service page", "CTA", "conversion"

## Core Principles

### The Fundamental Difference: B2B vs B2C

**B2B (Corporate Services):**
- Decision process: Rational, committee-based, risk-averse
- Cycle: Longer (weeks to months)
- Pain points: Business efficiency, ROI, professional reputation
- Tone: Authoritative but empathetic, data-driven
- Goal: Provide "logical ammunition" for the buyer to justify the purchase internally

**B2C (Local Physical Businesses):**
- Decision process: Emotional, personal, immediate
- Cycle: Shorter (hours to days)
- Pain points: Personal satisfaction, fear, status, convenience
- Tone: Sensory, aspirational, reassuring
- Goal: Overcome inertia and physical displacement friction

### Universal Psychological Triggers

1. **Loss Aversion (FOMO)** - People fear losing more than they desire gaining
2. **Social Proof** - Testimonials, numbers, logos reduce uncertainty
3. **Authority & Credibility** - Demonstrate expertise without jargon
4. **Scarcity & Urgency** - Limited time/availability (must be authentic)
5. **Hope & Transformation** - Paint the "after" picture vividly

## Proven Copywriting Formulas

### 1. PAS (Problem - Agitate - Solution)
**Best for:** Health services, repair services, pain-point driven B2B

**Structure:**
```
Problem: Identify the specific pain point
"¿Te cuesta levantarte de la cama cada mañana por el dolor de espalda?"

Agitate: Intensify by showing consequences
"Ese dolor constante no solo te molesta a ti; te impide jugar con tus hijos, 
rendir en el trabajo, y si lo ignoras, podría llevarte a cirugía."

Solution: Present your service as the relief
"Nuestra terapia de descompresión espinal alivia la presión desde la primera 
visita, sin fármacos ni cirugía."
```

**Critical:** The Agitation must be specific and quantified (lost revenue, wasted time, impact on life/business).

### 2. BAB (Before - After - Bridge)
**Best for:** Testimonials, case studies, cold emails, aspirational services

**Structure:**
```
Before: Current frustrating state
"Cada mes perdías 40 horas gestionando facturas manualmente."

After: Ideal transformed state
"Imagina tener toda tu contabilidad automatizada, con informes al instante 
y cero errores humanos."

Bridge: Your service connects the two
"Nuestro software ERP para pymes hace exactamente eso, sin necesidad de 
contratar un equipo de contabilidad."
```

### 3. AIDA (Attention - Interest - Desire - Action)
**Best for:** Aspirational B2C (beauty, fitness, dining), long-form sales pages

**Structure:**
```
Attention: Disruptive headline
"El secreto de las celebridades para una piel de cristal ha llegado a Madrid"

Interest: Curious fact or data
"¿Sabías que el colágeno disminuye un 1% cada año después de los 20?"

Desire: Visualization of the result
"Imagina verte en el espejo con una piel firme, luminosa y rejuvenecida 
en solo 45 minutos."

Action: Clear instruction
"Reserva tu diagnóstico facial gratuito (valorado en €50) antes del viernes."
```

### 4. The 4 U's (For Headlines)
**Best for:** Ad copy, email subjects, hero sections

Every headline should be:
- **Urgent:** "Solo quedan 4 semanas para estar listo para el verano"
- **Unique:** "El único gimnasio en Madrid abierto 24/7 con entrenadores presenciales"
- **Ultra-specific:** "Pierde 5 cm de cintura en 21 días (sin dietas extremas)"
- **Useful:** "Guía gratuita: 5 ejercicios para eliminar el dolor lumbar en casa"

### 5. Challenger Sale (For High-Ticket B2B)
**Best for:** Consulting, agencies, complex services

**Choreography:**
```
1. The Warmer: Show you understand their world
2. The Reframe: Introduce a provocative insight they haven't considered
3. Rational Drowning: Present data that validates the reframe
4. Emotional Impact: Connect data to their personal reality (stress, career risk)
5. A New Way: Describe the ideal solution (abstractly)
6. Your Solution: Reveal your service as the embodiment of that "New Way"
```

**Example Opening:**
"La mayoría de agencias prometen más leads. Pero hemos descubierto que el 70% 
de los leads que generan nunca se convierten en clientes. El problema real no 
es la cantidad, es la cualificación. Y eso cambia todo..."

## Sector-Specific Strategies

### B2B Services (Consulting, Agencies, SaaS)

**Service Page Structure:**
1. **Problem Definition** - Start by validating their challenge
2. **Methodology (The "How")** - Make the intangible tangible by explaining your process step-by-step
3. **Deliverables vs Results Matrix** - Distinguish what you deliver vs what they achieve
4. **Case Studies** - Written as journalistic stories, not boring technical sheets

**Key Copy Principles:**
- Use "We understand..." statements to show empathy
- Provide data and ROI projections (reduce anxiety)
- Include "logical ammunition" they can use to justify internally
- Avoid jargon; use client-oriented language

**About Us Page:**
Follow StoryBrand: Position your company as the "Guide" helping the "Hero" (client) succeed.
```
Structure:
1. Empathy: "Entendemos lo difícil que es escalar sin perder calidad..."
2. Authority: "Por eso hemos ayudado a 47 empresas tecnológicas a aumentar 
   sus leads cualificados en promedio un 156%"
3. The Team: Show how each expert solves client problems (not just titles)
```

### B2C Health & Wellness (Clinics, Physiotherapy, Dental)

**De-medicalize the Copy:**
Transform technical procedures into life benefits.

| Technical | Benefit-Oriented |
|-----------|------------------|
| "Implantes oseointegrados" | "Vuelve a comer tus alimentos favoritos con confianza y sonríe sin complejos" |
| "Tratamiento de conducto" | "Alivio inmediato para tu dolor de muelas y salvación de tu diente natural" |
| "Ajuste quiropráctico" | "Elimina el dolor que te impide vivir plenamente" |

**Microcopy for Forms:**
Buttons: Change "Reservar" → "Solicitar mi visita sin dolor"
Near CTA: "Sedación disponible" | "Primera consulta gratuita" | "Ambiente libre de juicios"

**Landing Page Focus:**
- Address fear and pain directly
- Use "Conditions Treated" pages (specific pain points) vs generic "Services"
- Offer low-commitment entry: "Examen inicial por €29 (Valor €150)"

### B2C Beauty & Fitness (Gyms, Salons, Med Spas)

**Transformation Copy:**
Don't sell the product (Botox), sell the result ("Recupera tu frescura natural").

**Combat "Gymtimidation":**
Focus on community, not equipment.
"No eres un número, eres parte de la familia. Entrena en un ambiente libre de 
juicios donde celebramos cada victoria, sin importar tu nivel."

**Stylist Bios:**
Tell stories, not just certifications.
"María se especializó en rubios platino durante su formación en Londres. 
Su pasión es devolver la vida a cabellos dañados por decoloración."

**Service Descriptions:**
Transform boring lists into experiences.
Bad: "Corte de mujer - €50"
Good: "Tu cita incluye consulta personalizada de estilo, lavado relajante con 
masaje craneal, corte de precisión y peinado final para que salgas lista para brillar."

### B2C Hospitality (Restaurants, Bars)

**Menu Engineering - Sensory Descriptions:**

| Generic | Sensory & Storytelling |
|---------|------------------------|
| "Salmón a la parrilla" | "Salmón noruego salvaje, asado a la perfección sobre brasas de roble, con un glaseado de miel de romero local" |
| "Tarta de chocolate" | "Decadente torta de chocolate belga oscuro (70% cacao), con un corazón fundido de caramelo salado casero" |

**Event Packages - Vision Selling:**
Don't just list capacity and prices. Paint the scene.
"Imagina celebrar tus 40 rodeado de tus amigos más cercanos en nuestra terraza 
privada al atardecer, con cócteles de autor en mano y la ciudad a tus pies."

## Landing Page Anatomy (Local Services)

**Hero Section (Above the Fold):**
```html
H1: [Service] en [Location]: [Primary Benefit] en [Timeframe]
Example: "Fisioterapia Deportiva en Madrid Centro: Elimina tu dolor 
y vuelve a entrenar en 3 sesiones"

Subtitle: Address main objection or add urgency
"Primera consulta gratuita. Aceptamos seguros. Citas disponibles hoy."

Hero Visual: Real photo of your location/team (NO stock photos)

CTA Button: Specific action + value
"Reservar mi Evaluación Gratuita" (not "Enviar")
```

**Body Sections (in order):**
1. **Problem/Solution** (PAS formula)
2. **Social Proof** (embedded Google reviews, client logos, press mentions)
3. **Benefits over Features** (use icons/bullet points)
4. **How It Works** (3-4 step process with visuals)
5. **FAQ** (address logistics: parking, insurance, preparation, what to bring)
6. **Final CTA** (repeat with different framing)
7. **Map + NAP** (Name, Address, Phone - critical for local SEO)

## Microcopy & Form Optimization

### CTA Button Best Practices

Bad: "Enviar"
Better: "Solicitar Cita"
Best: "Reservar mi Consulta Gratuita" or "Ver Horarios Disponibles"

**Click Triggers** (text below button):
- "No se requiere tarjeta de crédito"
- "Respuesta en menos de 1 hora"
- "Cancelación gratuita hasta 24h antes"

### Form Field Labels

Use human language that explains *why* you need the info:

| Generic | Optimized |
|---------|-----------|
| "Teléfono" | "Tu móvil (para confirmar la cita)" |
| "Comentarios" | "¿Hay algo más que debamos saber sobre tu lesión?" |
| "Email" | "Tu email (te enviaremos la confirmación aquí)" |

### Form Strategy

**Breadcrumbing (Multi-step forms):**
Start with easy, low-commitment questions before asking for personal data.
1. "¿Qué tipo de servicio necesitas?" (radio buttons)
2. "¿Cuándo te gustaría visitarnos?" (calendar)
3. NOW ask for: Name, Email, Phone

This uses the principle of consistency - once they start, they're more likely to finish.

## Cold Email Templates (B2B)

### Structure for High-Response Outbound

**Subject Line:**
- Question format: "¿Pregunta sobre [Process]?"
- Curiosity: "Idea para [Company Name]"
- Direct: "[Mutual Contact] sugirió que habláramos"

**Body (PAS/AIDA hybrid):**
```
The Hook (Personalized Research):
"Vi tu post en LinkedIn sobre expandir el equipo de ventas en [Company]..."

The Pitch (Problem + Social Proof):
"Muchas empresas SaaS en tu etapa enfrentan el reto de que sus AEs pasan 
más tiempo en admin que vendiendo. Ayudamos a [Similar Company] a automatizar 
su follow-up y aumentaron las demos agendadas un 40%."

Low-Commitment CTA (Interest-based):
"¿Te interesaría ver cómo lo hicimos? Puedo enviarte el caso de estudio."

[NOT: "¿Cuándo nos reunimos?" - too much friction]
```

**Follow-up Cadence:**
- Day 1: Initial email (value-driven)
- Day 3: Send a useful resource (article/case study)
- Day 7: Different angle (new insight)
- Day 14: "Break-up email" - "Supongo que no es prioritario ahora, ¿debería volver a escribir en 6 meses?"

## Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

Translate features into the "job" the customer is hiring your service to do.

**Formula for Headlines:**
"Ayúdame a [Job] para que pueda [Goal] evitando [Barrier]"

**Example:**
"Libérate de la microgestión de facturas (Barrera) para enfocarte en la 
estrategia de crecimiento (Job) y asegurar la rentabilidad este trimestre (Goal)."

## Social Proof & Trust Stack

### The Trust Stack (Landing Pages)

Group multiple credibility indicators in one dense section "above the fold":
1. **Positioning Headline:** "La agencia de marketing digital más premiada en Madrid"
2. **Client Logos:** 6-8 recognizable brands in your sector
3. **Quantified Data:** "+500 campañas exitosas" | "€2M+ en retorno para clientes"
4. **Authority Testimonial:** One powerful quote from a high-profile client

### Case Study Narrative Structure

Write as journalistic stories, not boring datasheets.

**Template:**
```
Headline: "Cómo [Company] ahorró €50K en [Timeframe] sin despedir a nadie"

The Hero: [Client] (not your company)
The Conflict: Describe the acute, relatable problem
The Resolution: How your intervention changed the course
Hard Data: Specific, numerical results (%, €, time saved)
```

## A/B Testing Priorities

Test high-impact elements first:

1. **Headlines:** Pain-focused vs Benefit-focused
2. **CTA Copy:** Generic vs Value-specific
3. **Specificity:** Round numbers vs Exact numbers
   - "Más de 100 clientes" vs "117 clientes satisfechos"
4. **Social Proof Position:** Before form vs After form

## Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Clarity Over Complexity (Basecamp/Stripe Style)

**Avoid:**
- Corporate jargon: "utilizar", "apalancar", "sinergia", "optimizar"
- Passive voice: "Se ha detectado un error"
- Complex words when simple ones work

**Use:**
- Active, conversational language: "usar", "aprovechar", "trabajo en equipo", "mejorar"
- Direct statements: "Encontramos un error"
- Write like you speak at dinner with friends

### Personality & Empathy (Mailchimp Style)

Recognize the human behind the screen is probably stressed or bored.
"Sabemos que odias hacer facturas. Por eso lo automatizamos todo."

## Pre-Flight Checklist

Before publishing any landing page or key copy, verify:

**Trust Signals:**
- [ ] Is social proof visible (reviews, logos, numbers)?
- [ ] Are testimonials specific (not "Great service!")?
- [ ] Is there a real photo of the team/location?

**Mobile Optimization:**
- [ ] Is the H1 readable on mobile?
- [ ] Are paragraphs short (2-3 lines max)?
- [ ] Is the CTA button big enough (min 44px height)?
- [ ] Is the phone number clickable (click-to-call)?

**Clarity:**
- [ ] Did I eliminate all jargon?
- [ ] Does the H1 answer: Service + Location + Benefit?
- [ ] Can a user understand the offer in 5 seconds?

**Conversion:**
- [ ] Is the CTA button visible without scrolling?
- [ ] Does the button use action + value words?
- [ ] Did I reduce form fields to the minimum?
- [ ] Is there a Google Map embedded?

**SEO Local:**
- [ ] Does the copy include neighborhood/city names?
- [ ] Is there an FAQ section?
- [ ] Are there "near me" relevant keywords?

## When NOT to Use This Skill

- Writing technical documentation
- Creating internal wikis or knowledge bases
- Academic or research papers
- Product descriptions for e-commerce (different optimization)
- Blog articles not focused on conversion

## Integration with Other Skills

This skill works best when combined with:
- **Frontend Design** - for creating high-converting landing pages
- **SEO** - for local search optimization
- **Email Marketing Tools** - for automation sequences
- **Analytics** - to measure copy performance (A/B tests)

---

**Remember:** Great copywriting is not about being clever or creative for its own sake. It's about clear communication that reduces friction, builds trust, and makes taking action irresistible.
